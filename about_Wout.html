I was born in Wilrijk on May 27th 1979 and am the big brother for my 3 brothers: Stijn, Niels-Jan and Florian. From 1991 to 1998 I went to secondary school (Science-Mathematics) at the â€˜Sint-Jozef college' in Turnhout.
I graduated in 2002 at the Free University of Brussels (VUB) with a Master in Biomedical Sciences. I completed my Master's internship in the Cell Differentiation Unit of the VUB Diabetes Centre under the supervision of Prof. L. Bouwens. My master thesis focused on the role of gastrin in pancreas regeneration through the CCK2 receptor. This is how I got fascinated about how cells decide to differentiate or trans-differentiate into a specific cell type. I found it very intriguing how a process which is essential for normal development could be related back to tumour development where a cell de-differentiates into a cancer cell.
Due to my other interest into informatics, I decided to complete a master's programme into Bioinformatics at the Catholic University of Leuven (KULeuven) to complement my scientific degree.
As a result of this, my professional career has been a mix of science and informatics work experiences. My research experience has mainly been focused on cancer research, with associate researcher work on breast cancer at the Leuven Cancer Institute (European Cancerdegradome project) and my doctoral dissertation on prostate cancer at the University of East Anglia under supervision of Prof. S. Fairweather-Tait. I have also contributed to a project looking into the molecular mechanisms underlying the tumour and metastasis-suppressive effects of MMP8 in breast cancer. My most recent research project (European SaveMe project) involves the role of the degradome in pancreatic cancer using 2D and 3D in vitro models. In addition, I have exploited my informatics skills to develop various research and medical databases which are further descibed on the website. I'm continuously working on my professional development and am currently expanding my knowledge on Webdesign.
